◆ Track 1

EdCity.ai — The Learning Companion Every Classroom Runs On

Not another AI tool teachers try once. The platform every school defaults to, the way they default to a textbook or a timetable.

🔍 Current observation
  • Teachers already reach for AI every day — the opportunity isn't introducing the tool, it's becoming the one they trust enough to build their whole practice around.4
  • Marking and feedback, done right, don't just save time — they change whether a student actually learns from the assignment. Staged release is today's one evidenced example of "done right," not the only possible one.
  • EdCity.ai already sits at the center of how teachers prep, mark, group, and communicate.
❤ Why this matters
  • Every hour reclaimed from admin is an hour returned to actual teaching
  • Every assignment a student is made to think through, not skip, compounds into real capability
  • The system that owns this daily habit owns the relationship with every teacher and student in Hong Kong

▲ Track 2

EdMarket — The Trust Engine Behind Every Tool Schools Adopt

The channel that turns "which AI tool should we even trust" into a solved problem for every school in Hong Kong.

🔍 Current observation
  • Vendors want distribution and credibility; schools want confidence they're not taking a risk on an unproven tool.
  • Today that trust gets built one relationship at a time — EdMarket turns it into a system every school can rely on.
  • Subject-leads already shape what gets purchased; Admin/IT already controls what gets switched on.5
❤ Why this matters
  • Every school that adopts through EdMarket instead of vetting alone saves real time and real risk
  • Every vendor that succeeds through EdMarket deepens Hong Kong's EdTech ecosystem, not just EdCity's own product
  • This is how EdCity becomes infrastructure for the whole sector, not just a single app

★ Track 3

A School Where No Student Learns Alone

EdCity's next frontier: making sure every student has companions in the pursuit, not just a tool that answers their questions.

🔍 Current observation
  • Learning that happens entirely alone rarely lasts — curiosity and effort are easier to sustain with others alongside you.6
  • Today's AI interaction is one student and one assistant; the social, shared side of learning has no home yet.
  • This is the frontier that separates a platform that raises scores from one that raises learners.
❤ Why this matters
  • As a government-backed platform, EdCity's mandate is bigger than exam results — joy and sustained curiosity are part of the outcome, not a bonus
  • This is the clearest way for EdCity to stand apart from generic AI-tutor products
  • Get this right and EdCity becomes known for developing whole learners, not just test scores

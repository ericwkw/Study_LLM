This table covers human and organizational actors only — the systems themselves (EdCity.ai, School Management System) are on the Systems relationships tab. Teacher (primary & secondary), student, EDB, and admin/IT touch all five maps — genuinely cross-cutting actors, and the ones most exposed if the four services don't share data or design language. Subject-lead is close behind at four. Everyone else is a single-service specialist (vendor, gatekeepers, content/SMS teams) — their needs are real but narrow, and probably shouldn't drive cross-service architecture decisions the way the cross-cutting actors' needs should. This table is itself still mostly theorized outside the EdCity.ai and EdMarket maps — treat the "appears in" counts as a structural hypothesis to test, not a settled fact.

Performance data layer

School Management System
Two data layers above, held within this one system
DE Resources Platform is the entry point that provides access to EdCity.ai; EdMarket access is mediated through EdCity.ai's tool section rather than a direct link from DE Resources Platform. School Management System is not one relationship — its official-records layer only pushes data out, while its performance-data layer both receives and returns data. Collapsing these into a single generic connection would lose that distinction.

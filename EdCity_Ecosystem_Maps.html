Teacher, student, and EDB touch all five maps — genuinely cross-cutting actors, and the ones most exposed if the four services don't share data or design language. Principal and subject-lead are close behind at four. Everyone else is a single-service specialist (vendor, gatekeepers, content/SMS teams) — their needs are real but narrow, and probably shouldn't drive cross-service architecture decisions the way teacher/student/EDB needs should. This table is itself still mostly theorized outside the AI Tools and EdMarket maps — treat the "appears in" counts as a structural hypothesis to test, not a settled fact.

↓EdCity.ai and other EdCity services connect to School Management System in two distinct ways
Official records layer
→ one-directional: feeds official student rosters into EdCity.ai and other EdCity services. Not written back to.
Performance data layer
↔ bidirectional: written to when students complete assignments/exercises via their own entry points in EdCity.ai, or by consuming other EdCity services; read back out by those same systems.
School Management System
Two data layers above, held within this one system
DE Resources Platform is the entry point that provides access to EdCity.ai; EdMarket access is mediated through EdCity.ai's tool section rather than a direct link from DE Resources Platform. School Management System is not one relationship — its official-records layer only pushes data out, while its performance-data layer both receives and returns data. Collapsing these into a single generic connection would lose that distinction.

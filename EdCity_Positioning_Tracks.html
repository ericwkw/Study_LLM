◆ Track 1

EdCity.ai as the Core Value Engine

Positioning EdCity.ai as the primary product, with the other three systems as supporting infrastructure around it.

🔍 Why (current observation)
  • Teacher AI adoption is already solved — the real gap is keeping students thinking, not introducing the tool.
  • Generic AI marking doesn't match pedagogy without staged release.
  • EdCity.ai is the only service with real evidence behind more than one actor relationship.
❤ Why it matters
  • Highest-confidence value in the whole map
  • Matches the confirmed architecture chain (Resources → EdCity.ai → EdMarket)
  • Directly serves the two mission-critical actors: teacher, student

▲ Track 2

EdMarket as Enabling Infrastructure

Real, evidenced value — but positioned as B2B plumbing behind EdCity.ai, not a headline value prop.

🔍 Why (current observation)
  • Vendor relationship and subject-lead purchasing influence are the best-evidenced findings in the whole map.
  • Admin/IT's subscription-approval role is prototype-confirmed.
  • Every actor here is a school-operations or partner role, not teacher/student.
❤ Why it matters
  • Leading with EdMarket risks framing EdCity as a marketplace, not a learning product
  • But under-selling it wastes real, confirmed value for schools and vendors

★ Track 3

Companionship & Beyond-Exam Learning

A named, currently-unaddressed opportunity — proposed direction only, nothing here is built or scoped yet.

🔍 Why (current observation)
  • All current AI interaction is 1:1 (student ↔ EdCity.ai) — no peer-facing mechanism exists.
  • Teaching groups are teacher-assigned, not peer-driven or social.
  • This is the clearest gap between EdCity's stated mission and its shipped product.
❤ Why it matters
  • As a gov-funded body, success isn't exam performance alone — joy and sustained engagement are coequal goals
  • Real differentiator if EdCity wants to lead with "more than exam performance"
  • Currently pure aspiration, not a shipped capability — flagged so it isn't mistaken for one

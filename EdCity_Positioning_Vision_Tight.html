◆ Track 1

EdCity.ai — The Learning Companion Every Classroom Runs On

Not another AI tool teachers try once. The platform every school defaults to, the way they default to a textbook or a timetable.

Pain points → Stakes
🔍 Pain points
  • Teachers lose real hours to prep and marking, under constant time pressure.4
  • AI feedback shown too early lets students skip the thinking an assignment was meant to build.
  • Students are tempted to use AI as a shortcut to the answer, not a tool for learning.
  • Parents receive progress numbers with no sense of what to actually do with them.
❤ Why it matters
  • Time lost to admin is time not spent teaching
  • A shortcut taken today is a skill never built — and it compounds every year
  • If AI erodes what a mark actually means, trust — and adoption — stalls exactly where it matters most
  • Parents disconnected from real progress can't meaningfully support their child at home

▲ Track 2

EdMarket — The Trust Engine Behind Every Tool Schools Adopt

The channel that turns "which AI tool should we even trust" into a solved problem for every school in Hong Kong.

Pain points → Stakes
🔍 Pain points
  • Vendors struggle to get credible distribution into schools without deep existing relationships.5
  • Subject-leads have real purchasing influence but no systematic way to evaluate tools consistently.
  • Admin/IT manages subscriptions and enforcement manually, one request at a time.
  • Schools risk adopting unproven tools — or missing good ones — without a trusted vetting layer.
❤ Why it matters
  • Without a trusted channel, good tools stay small and weak ones spread unchecked
  • Manual, ad hoc vetting means risk and inconsistency scale with every new tool on the market
  • Every hour Admin/IT spends on manual subscription work is an hour not spent on higher-value support

★ Track 3

A School Where No Student Learns Alone

EdCity's next frontier: making sure every student has companions in the pursuit, not just a tool that answers their questions.

Pain points → Stakes
🔍 Pain points
  • Students interact with AI largely alone — there's no peer-facing dimension to how they learn today.6
  • Teaching groups are assigned by teachers, not shaped by students' own curiosity or connection.
  • A platform focused only on marks and mastery risks losing what makes learning worth sustaining.
❤ Why it matters
  • Learning attempted in isolation is harder to sustain — motivation fades faster without shared effort
  • Optimizing only for scores risks producing compliance, not the joy and curiosity central to EdCity's own mission
  • This is the one dimension of learning no generic AI-tutor product is built to address

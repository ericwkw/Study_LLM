On the thinking-preservation mechanism: where it appears below, it refers to a mechanism designed to encourage active student thinking before AI help arrives — not one fixed, definitive design. What this suggests for positioning

  • EdCity.ai is the center of gravity. Hero service for 5 of 15 actors, and the only service with genuinely evidenced value beyond a single relationship. Positioning it as the primary product — with DE Resources Platform, SMS, and EdMarket as supporting infrastructure — matches both the confirmed architecture (DE Resources Platform → EdCity.ai → EdMarket) and where the evidence actually lives.
  • EdMarket’s value is real but narrow and B2B-shaped — it matters most to vendor, subject-lead, Admin/IT, and Principal, none of whom are EdCity’s mission-critical actors (teachers, students). A better fit for “enabling infrastructure” positioning than a headline value prop.
  • DE Resources Platform and SMS read as infrastructure, not value drivers — every actor’s relationship to them is indirect or thinly evidenced. Not necessarily wrong, but neither should anchor an external-facing value narrative alone.
  • The clearest unaddressed value gap is peer/companionship learning — named by the belief system (joy/companionship as coequal to exam performance), with zero current mechanism in the build. A real candidate differentiator for “more than exam performance” positioning, but aspirational, not shipped.
  • Primary teacher and the entire governance/partner column (EDB, gatekeepers, content/SMS/academic partner) are the thinnest evidence in the map. Treat their “hero service” assignments as working hypotheses, not validated claims — they need real research before driving roadmap or positioning decisions, the way secondary-teacher and vendor findings already have.

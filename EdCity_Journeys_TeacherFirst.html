Teacher journeys — a deep dive

One actor, mapped in full. Instead of a single need → friction → interaction → outcome line per persona, every real moment in a teacher’s week with EdCity gets its own journey, its own pain points, and its own service mapping — because depth, not breadth, is the point of this document.

Evidenced — real field/vendor research or confirmed prototype behavior
Partly evidenced — real underlying fact, specifics inferred or extrapolated
Theorized — happy-path proposal, no research behind it
Services: EdCity.ai DE Resources Platform EdMarket School Management System. Naming follows the confirmed glossary used across the EdCity ecosystem maps.
On the thinking-preservation mechanism: where it appears below, it refers to a mechanism designed to encourage active student thinking before AI help arrives, used by the teacher — staged feedback release is today’s one evidenced example, not the only or definitive design.

What depth buys the teacher

Thirteen distinct moments, three teacher personas, mapped in full rather than compressed into one line each. The bet: a teacher who trusts every one of these moments builds a daily practice around EdCity — that trust compounds faster than a thinner layer spread across more actors ever could.
Prep & materials
Real hours saved every week, validated against the curriculum a teacher already has to teach to.
Assignments & marking
A thinking-preservation mechanism a teacher controls, not a black box she has to trust blindly.
Insight & calibration
A class-wide picture, with department-wide consistency still an open gap worth naming honestly.
Confidence from first use
A first experience built for her subject and grade, not a generic prompt box.
Safety she doesn’t verify herself
Claimed, not yet built — the one moment in this map still resting entirely on theorized ground.
Discovery without the vetting burden
Specialist tools arrive pre-vetted, one click away, inside the platform she already trusts.
